Introduction¶

In this study, it is aimed to figure out what kind of relationship exists between the variables to be processed and visualize the data to see their behaviour with respect to time. USD/TRY exchange rate, tourist numbers and average expenditure per capita, which are received from "EVDS", and search statistics of "Turkey" in travel category, which is received from "Google Trends", are used in this study. All data is monthly and they represents between 2011 and 2020.

To understand the time related relations line graphs, histograms and box plots are used for each of data. Moreover, to identify the relation between variables, correlation plot is used.

Correlation¶

In [195]:
#Correlation 
correl_info = cor(the_data[c(2,3,4,5,7)])
ggcorrplot(correl_info, 
           hc.order = TRUE, 
           type = "lower",
           lab = TRUE)

First thing that is conspicuous is, there is strong corelation between search rates of "Turkey" and tourist numbers which is reasonable, since they have similar seasonality. In addition, there is a strong negative correlation between dolar rate and average spending. As mentioned before, this is a result of that value of Turkish Liras is exponentially decreasing against USD. They can purchase same amount of services or products, while spending less amount of money in terms of USD. And, again as a result of USD rates changing, as expected, there is respectively strong correlation between dolar rate and smoothed tourist numbers. This change in USD rates, made Turkey more attractive and inexpensive choice for tourists.

Conclusion¶

To sum up, I chose three different monthly data from EVDS which are Tourist Numbers, USD/TRY Exchange Rate and, Average Expenditure per Capita, and chose a data about "Turkey" search rates in travel category from Google Trends. The aim while choosing these data was to see if there is a seasonality, trends and existence of the possible relationship between those. It is turned out that Tourist Numbers and "Turkey" Search Rates and, Average Expenditure per Capita have seasonality. USD/TRY exchange probably has exponential upward trend. On the other hand, while dolar rate has strong negative correlation between average expenditure and positive correlation between smoothed tourist numbers, search statistics of "Turkey" has strong relation between tourist numbers.
